OVM Dive Planner Operating Manual

This document is a step-by-step operating guide for divers using OVM Dive Planner. It is intended for direct practical use rather than marketing or overview reading.

Important:

Review every calculated schedule manually before relying on it. Warning messages, gas selections, and settings should be checked for each dive plan.

1. Startup and General Navigation

The app has four tabs:

  • Plan for dive setup
  • Schedule for calculated results
  • Mixer for gas calculations
  • Settings for planner-wide defaults

Before planning a dive, review the settings at least once to confirm units, rates, and gradient factors.

2. Creating an Open-Circuit Plan

Step 1

Open the Plan tab and set Circuit to Open Circuit.

Step 2

Select Visual or List planner mode.

Step 3

Enter the profile using either waypoints or list levels.

Step 4

Select the bottom gas. You may use a preset or define the gas manually by O₂ and He percentage.

Step 5

Add deco gases if needed. Leave switch depth blank for automatic handling, or set it manually.

Step 6

Tap Plan Dive and then inspect the Schedule tab.

3. Creating a CCR Plan

Step 1

Open the Plan tab and set Circuit to CCR.

Step 2

Use the bottom gas section as the diluent gas entry.

Step 3

Open CCR Settings… and define low, high, and deco setpoints plus the high-setpoint switch depth.

Step 4

Add bailout/deco gases in the CCR settings sheet if required.

Step 5

Calculate the dive and inspect both the primary CCR schedule and the bailout section in the results.

4. Using the Visual Planner

  • Tap the chart to add a waypoint.
  • Drag a waypoint vertically to change depth.
  • Edit hold time using the controls listed below the chart.
  • Use the delete control to remove a waypoint when needed.
  • The chart scales dynamically based on the dive profile.

5. Using the List Planner

  • Each row is one level with depth and time.
  • Use Add Level to extend the profile.
  • Levels are processed from top to bottom.
  • Transit between levels uses the rates defined in settings.

6. Reviewing Results

After every calculation, review:

  • total runtime
  • bottom runtime
  • first stop depth
  • total stop time
  • decompression schedule
  • gas usage
  • CNS and OTU values
  • all warnings shown at the top of the results page

If the app reports warnings, do not ignore them. Re-check gases, switch depths, setpoints, and settings.

7. Planning Repetitive Dives

  1. Expand the Repetitive Dive section in the Plan tab.
  2. Enable repetitive-dive planning.
  3. Enter the surface interval.
  4. Define Dive 2 profile and gases.
  5. Calculate and review both schedules in the results tab.

8. Using the Gas Mixer

  • Tank Fill (Boost): donor and target tank transfer
  • Top-Off Blend: final gas after topping off an existing mix
  • Trimix Mix: required O₂, He, and air fill sequence for a target mix

Review pressure units and percentages carefully before using the calculated values for actual blending.

9. Pre-Dive Verification Checklist

  1. Confirm circuit type is correct.
  2. Confirm units are correct.
  3. Confirm water type and surface pressure are correct.
  4. Confirm gradient factors and last stop depth are correct.
  5. Confirm bottom gas or diluent is correct.
  6. Confirm deco or bailout gases are correct.
  7. Confirm switch depths are correct or intentionally left automatic.
  8. Review all warnings.
  9. Review total runtime and gas usage.
  10. Independently verify the plan before use.
